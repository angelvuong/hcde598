Assignment 1: Laser Cut Press Fit Construction Kit

I created a press kit inspired by concrete breeze block screens that were popularized in American mid-century architecture.

Collaborators:
Connor Stein, M Arch - Thank you for the time you spent helping me learn Rhino and for letting me use your scrap cardboard!
Liang He, CS PhD - Thank you for showing me MakerCase!

Iteration 1-4

I started this project by designing a block layout in Illustrator. I used a template that I generated in MakerCase. I was inspired by the "Empress" breeze block pattern and incorporated it into my design by creating simple shapes in Illustrator. Iteration 1 (13% power, 9% speed) my first iteration turned out well! Connected with finger slots, the box stood on its own but collapsed with gentle shaking. I used E-Flute cardboard.
Iteration 2 (14% power,9% speed) I attempted to decrease the size of notches for a tighter fit but the pieces would not fit together.
Iteration 3 (25% power, 10% speed) used T-slot connectors but could not stand on its own and frequently collapsed.
Iteration 4 (25% power, 10% speed) Tested with C-flute cardboard and did not like the bulky look of the cuts.
